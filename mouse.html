Introduction to Mouse

The term "mouse" was first used in 1965 in Bill English's "Computer-Aided Display Control" due to its resemblance to a mouse and its cord resembling its tail. The term also came about because the cursor on the screen was referred to as "CAT" and was seen as chasing the new desktop device. Types of mouse

Telefunken introduced their early ball mouse in 1968, sold as optional computer system equipment. Bill English created a ball mouse in 1972 while working for Xerox PARC. It replaced external wheels with a single, rotating ball, and became the predominant form used in personal computers. Xerox PARC also adopted a modern keyboard layout.Read More...

Conclusion

In 1988, the VTech Socrates educational video game console introduced a wireless mouse as an optional controller. In the early 1990s, the Super Nintendo Entertainment System and Nintendo 64 also featured a mouse. Sega, NEC, Sony, Nintendo, and Microsoft all released official mice for their consoles, including the PlayStation, Wii, and Xbox.
